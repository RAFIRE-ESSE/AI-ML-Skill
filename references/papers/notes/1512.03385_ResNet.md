- **Title**: Deep Residual Learning for Image Recognition
- **Authors**: Kaiming He, Xiangyu Zhang, Shaoqing Ren, Jian Sun
- **Year**: 2015 (CVPR 2016 Best Paper)
- **arXiv**: [1512.03385](https://arxiv.org/abs/1512.03385)

## Key Innovation

Solves the **degradation problem** in ultra-deep neural networks (where adding more layers causes higher training error due to vanishing/exploding gradients) by introducing **residual shortcut connections**.

---

## Formulations

Instead of fitting target mapping $H(x)$, residual blocks explicitly fit residual mapping $F(x) = H(x) - x$:

$$\mathcal{H}(x) = \mathcal{F}(x, \{W_i\}) + x$$

- Identity shortcut $x$ adds zero additional parameters and zero extra computational complexity.
- Allows gradients to flow directly back through shortcut connections to early layers during backpropagation.
